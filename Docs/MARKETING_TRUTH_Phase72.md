## 1. What changed under the marketing (the honest new pitch)

Hearthbound Hollow is no longer a 75-minute authored corridor. It is a **cozy daily practice**:

- A **morning Agenda** you wake into, a **Request Board** of villagers that never empties, a
  **shop you grow** with coin, a **garden** you tend and brew from, a **workbench** with varied,
  gently-masterable craft, and a **Memory Wall** of Echo threads you choose to complete.
- A **compounding loop** (coin → upgrades & garden → capacity → memories → coin) wrapped around
  the writing that was always the prize.

## 2. Positioning guidance (what the store page should and shouldn't say)

**Do say:**
- *"A cozy memory-keeper's practice: run your Hollow day by day."*
- *"A village of people who need you — a different few each morning."*
- *"Grow your shop, tend your garden, complete the Echo Web, keep the village's memories."*
- *"Wholly opt-in and unpunishing: no fail states, no timers, kindness is always optimal."*

**Don't say (refund drivers):**
- Don't hard-anchor to *"Stardew Valley"* as a feature promise. Use the *tone* comparison
  honestly (*"Stardew's warmth × Spiritfarer's weight"*) but lead with the **memory-broker hook**,
  which is the freshest thing in cozy.
- Don't imply combat, competition, or twitch skill. The craft is gentle by design.
- Don't quote an hours figure the content doesn't yet back. Quote the *loop*, not a number.
