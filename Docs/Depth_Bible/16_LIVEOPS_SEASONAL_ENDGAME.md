## 3.0 The Two Paid Story DLCs

Adopted from original GAME_DESIGN.md + [REV § 6].

### 3.1 DLC #1 — *The Travelers* (Year 2, Q1)

**Price: $14.99.**

**Content:**
- 4 new caravan-tier visitors who arrive from the rural valleys (NOT from the City of Wells).
- Each visitor stays for 7–14 in-game days.
- Each has their own memory map (6–8 memories each).
- 1 new Drifter village template.
- The Caravan Stop is expanded into a small explorable area.
- 12+ hours of content.

**Why it works:** the visitors are *welcome guests* to the cozy player. They are not threats. They bring stories from outside without breaking Embershade's containment.

### 3.2 DLC #2 — *The Keeper Before You* (Year 3, Q2)

**Price: $14.99.**

**Content:**
- The full Marin Vell story, told from her perspective.
- A 12–16 hour standalone narrative campaign that *precedes* the main game.
- The player plays as Marin in the years leading to her dispersal.
- All 22 of Marin's hand-sealed memories are made playable.
- New endings: *did Marin disperse, or did she choose differently?* (Some endings lead to a Hearthbound Hollow that never opened. Cozy alternate-history.)
- Unlocks special hologram messages in the main game.

**Why this is the cozy game's deepest pull:** it is *the prequel that fans of Marin will buy without question.* Modeled on Pentiment's narrative DLC and Citizen Sleeper 2's expanded narratives.

### 3.3 Why no City-of-Wells DLC

The Codex 03 § 3.2 discipline holds. The City never appears. This is the bible's most disciplined refusal.

## 4.0 The Greenlight Gate (the most important pre-launch artifact)

(Adopted from [REV § 6 R-2] verbatim.)

### 4.1 The vertical-slice scope

Build a fully-finished slice containing:

- **Doris Vance — all 14 memories, fully hand-written and dossier-validated.**
- **2 of her 14 memories made into Long Dreams** (full $18k illustrated each).
- **Polish + Cleanse mini-games** at production quality.
- **The Echo Hologram's first message** (MAR-001).
- **Pickle's voice acting samples** (4 quotes).
- **The Lantern Walk festival** (one festival, fully playable).
- **The Confession Booth opening sequence.**
- **The Forgotten Year arc's opening beat** (Ren visits the shop, says he is missing someone).

### 4.2 The 200-person paid playtest

200 cozy-target users (recruited via cozy creator partnerships, paid $30 each = $6,000 incentive budget + recruitment costs ~$8,000).

The playtest:

1. Each player plays the slice for 4–6 hours.
2. Each completes a structured survey.
3. Each is interviewed for 20 minutes after.
4. Telemetry tracked.

### 4.3 The gating criterion

> **70% of playtesters must say "I would pre-order this game" with no reservation.**
>
> If <60% say so: project pivots (per [REV § 6 R-2 fallback]).
>
> If 60–70% say so: project goes through a 3-month polish + re-test cycle.
>
> If 70%+: full production begins.

### 4.4 The pivot fallback

If the playtest fails:

The project becomes a *5-villager A Short Hike-tradition cozy narrative* — 80% writing budget eliminated, 50% revenue ceiling retained. The studio ships a smaller, beloved cozy game in 14 months and lives to make its next.

This is the **anti-bankruptcy escape hatch.** A studio that takes this seriously cannot lose its identity to one project.

(Cross-referenced from Codex 15 § 5.)

### 6.1 The 16-month rollout

| Time | Beat |
|---|---|
| Launch | The ARG is in the game. No public mention. |
| Launch + 2 months | First wave of community awareness — players start sharing "MAR-011" finds online. |
| Launch + 4 months | Studio acknowledges the ARG on socials. Newsletter feature. |
| Launch + 6 months | Threshold likely hits 1,000 assemblies. *The First Reveal* fires. |
| Launch + 10 months | The Second Reveal Threshold (5,000 assemblies). *The Second Reveal* — adds the Sommelier path opening for all (Codex 16 § 7). |

| Launch + 16 months | The DLC2 "The Keeper Before You" launches, *building on* the third reveal. |

### 6.2 Why this works

The ARG is **free engagement marketing.** Every reveal triggers a wave of community discussion + creator content. The DLC2 launch lands in a community already primed for Marin's story.

### 6.3 The offline-player path

Solo / offline players experience all revealed content at a deferred-unlock — about 18 months after each reveal, the content is added to the base game offline. **Nobody is permanently locked out.** This is Codex 06's discipline.

## 7.0 The Memory Sommelier Endgame

The post-credits, 100-hour endgame layer for the cozy player who refuses to leave.

### 7.1 What it is

After any ending, the player can pursue the **Memory Sommelier path** — a profession-tier specialization that elevates Pickle's tasting commentary into a full mechanic the player can perform.

### 7.2 Mechanics

The player learns to **taste memories themselves.** This unlocks:

- **A vocabulary system** — the player can write taste-notes on memories (a UI-text input system that uses validated terms from a dictionary of 200+ taste-descriptors).
- **A reputation track** — the Sommelier's Trust meter, separate from the Hollow's Reputation.
- **Auction sponsorships** — the player can be invited to evaluate other players' Auction memories.
- **The Sommelier's Spoon** (Codex 04 § 4 — Memory Mastery max reward). A cosmetic in-shop item, gold-rimmed, displayed on a stand.
- **A small income stream** — the player earns Coin for evaluating others' Auction memories on the global Auction.

### 7.3 The Sommelier path's writing budget

~25,000 words of new content. ~6 weeks of senior writer time. Released as **part of the +12-month free update** (Codex 16 § 2 — *The Apprentice's Year*).

### 7.4 Why this is novel

No cozy game has shipped a **post-credits profession-tier specialization with vocabulary-based player input.** Mind Scanners gestured at it; Hearthbound Hollow delivers it as a 100-hour rabbit hole.

| Symptom | Response |

| Refund rate >10% | Activate Gentle Mode as default + Tone Compass push + immediate hotfix pass |

## 12.0 Cozy LiveOps as Counter-Culture

The cozy game's LiveOps is **deliberately the opposite** of mainstream LiveOps:

| Mainstream LiveOps | Cozy LiveOps (this bible) |
|---|---|
| Battle passes | Free seasonal updates |
| FOMO timers | Always-available content |
| Currency packs | One-time purchase; no real-money currencies |
| Daily login rewards | Soft Days (opt-in, capped) |
| Aggressive notifications | Monthly newsletter (opt-in) |
| Live events with player competition | Communal screenings + memory exchange |
| Engagement metrics as primary KPI | Refund rate + recommendation rate as primary KPI |

This is **the contract.** Breaking any line above breaks the brand. **No exceptions.**
