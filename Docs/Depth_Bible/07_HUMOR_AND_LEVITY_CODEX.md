# Codex 07 — **Humor & Levity Codex**
### Specialist: **Mochi Tannenbaum, Humor & Levity Designer**
*(12 yrs · senior writer on Cult of the Lamb, contributor on Untitled Goose Game, Donut County, animation writer on Adventure Time s4-7)*

> *"The original pitch is gorgeous and devastating and reads like a thesis on grief. I cried twice reading it. That is exactly why it cannot be the only register. Spiritfarer's mid-game retention drops at the moment players run out of emotional gas. Cult of the Lamb's success is partly because it's funny. Cozy is not the same thing as sad. Cozy can be funny. Cozy can be **weird**. The cozy player wants to laugh out loud at least once per session. That is my job."*

---

## 1.0 Mission

Add a dedicated humor architecture to Hearthbound Hollow that:

1. **Operates at the rate of ≥1 laugh-out-loud beat per 25-minute session** (~one in-game day).
2. **Never undercuts grief.** Comedy is *protective ballast*, not comic relief that breaks tone.
3. **Has at least 5 sources running in parallel** so no single source feels exhausted.
4. **Is fully togglable** (Pell's request — Codex 06) but defaults to *on at moderate intensity.*
5. **Generates social-media moments** that are organically clip-worthy.

This codex catalogues **200+ codified comedy beats** across 7 humor systems. It also locks down voice rules and the boundary between cozy humor and slapstick.

## 3.0 Pickle's Voice Bible

Pickle the cat is the **single most important comedic character** in the game. Pickle is also genuinely useful (taste-tests memories — Codex 12 § 4). Pickle's voice signature is:

### 3.1 Pickle's voice rules

1. **Pickle is sarcastic but never mean to the player.** Sarcasm targets the world, never your choices.
2. **Pickle speaks in complete sentences with mock-grandeur.** "I have decided not to sit on the chair today. The chair has not earned me."
3. **Pickle is older than they look. Pickle remembers the predecessor.** Comedy + lore vehicle.
4. **Pickle is a wine critic.** All memory tastings are written in the register of an oenophile. *("This memory has a long finish. Notes of regret. A faint banana on the back-palate. Probably 1986. Definitely a Tuesday.")*
5. **Pickle is correct ~88% of the time** about memories. The 12% of incorrect readings are themselves jokes (the wrong reading is funnier than the right one would have been).
6. **Pickle does not respect rank.** Pickle interrupts the Mayor. Pickle reviews the Magistrate's posture. Pickle judges Brother Anselm's sandals.
7. **Pickle never speaks in the player's presence to NPCs.** Pickle's commentary is private to the player. (This is the cozy decorum.)

## 5.0 Memory Hayfever & Workshop Mishaps

### 5.1 The Hayfever mechanic

If the player over-cleanses memories without resting, they develop **Memory Hayfever**: a low-grade in-fiction allergy where they involuntarily *sneeze* small fragments of cleansed memories.

- Visible as a small particle effect.
- Plays a short, randomized one-sentence ghost-voice that quotes the cleansed memory's original phrasing.
- Pickle reacts: *"Bless you. You sneezed a stranger again."*
- Forces the player to take a Soft Day (or brew a sage-lavender tea) to recover.

This is **the cozy game's first allergy mechanic**. It is comedic, mechanical, and gently educational about cleansing safety.

## 6.0 The Vance Ashby Comedy Engine

Vance is the game's antagonist (Codex 03 § 8, Codex 05 § 8). He is also **funny.** This is the critical design choice that prevents him from feeling melodramatic.

### 6.1 Vance's comedic voice signature

1. He is **unfailingly polite.** He never raises his voice. His threats are dressed in compliments.
2. He has **corporate vocabulary** in a pre-industrial village. *"Memory-rich households," "extraction capacity," "soft inventory," "the long tail of grief."*
3. He **mispronounces villager names**, gently, throughout — and *they correct him*, gently, throughout. Comedy of escalating quiet exasperation.
4. He **takes Pickle seriously.** "Excellent assistant. Very board-meeting energy." Pickle hates him.
5. He **sincerely believes he is helping.** This is the most important rule. He is not a villain; he is a true believer in his bad idea. **Every line is funny in service of that.**

### 6.2 Sample Vance lines (10 of 30)

> *"The valley's memory-asset base is, frankly, undervalued. I'm not here to take anything from anyone. I'm here to *unlock latent emotional capital.* Do try the tea."*

> *"Mrs. Vance — *Vance Vance,* yes, common confusion, no relation — operates a delightful little brokerage in Greater Wells. We have something of an empire of feeling, between us. Have you considered franchising?"*

> *"Of course the villagers have the right to refuse. I am offering them a choice. A choice between holding onto a memory that, by my actuarial reading, is a net liability — and accepting fair-market compensation in city coin. I am, in a sense, the question."*

> *"Your cat is *charming.* She has the alertness of a small chief executive."*

> *"Your shop has — and I mean this kindly — the operational sophistication of a tea cozy. I would like to help. I have a presentation. It is ninety slides. They are illustrated."*

> *"I have, in my time, listed seventeen valleys. None of them objected. They are, even now, *thriving* — in the limited and depressing sense in which a place that has sold its history can be said to thrive."*

> *(at the Tribunal:)* *"I respect process. Indeed, I helped design the process at Wells. Your process is, if I may say, *adorable.* Your process and our process are friends across a great civilizational distance."*

> *(when refused:)* *"That is a very moving speech, Keeper. May I have a copy of it for marketing purposes?"*

> *(when tricked, after the player sells him compost memories:)* *"I see. I see. I will pay for these. I will not be paid for these. This is the most expensive lesson I have ever charged to my own account."*

> *(in his departing letter:)* *"I do not believe you have heard the last of me. I believe you have, however, won the present round. I salute you. — V."*

Vance's comedic register is **the satirical conscience of the game.** Players will quote him on TikTok. He is the antagonist they screenshot.

## 7.0 Idris the Bard — Bad-Verse Comedy

Idris is the wandering musician (Codex 02 § 4). He stays at the inn during caravan weeks. He believes in his own verse. He is not as good a poet as he thinks. **The comedy is in the gap.**

### 7.1 Sample Idris verses (5)

> *"O Doris of the Bees / Whose honey is the / Sticky kind."*

> *"The Mayor walks the cobblestones / In boots / In boots / In boots that are also boots."*

> *"I have loved many a road and have been loved / By approximately one road / In return."*

> *"Pickle, oh Pickle / I would like to compose for you / If only you would stop / Compositionally / Looking at me."*

> *"Forgive me, my friends, my dear friends in the Vale / I had a song about this / I forget how it ends / Probably bittersweetly."*

Idris's verses occasionally — *occasionally* — land. Once per playthrough, on the right Long-Night Festival evening, in the right tea-fueled mood, he writes the song that becomes the village's lullaby. This is the cozy game's moment of *the bad poet who one day was a good poet.* It is funny and devastating in series.

## 8.0 The Codex Marginalia (UI-level humor)

The Codex is the in-game UI lorebook. It has **marginalia** — small notes in the corners, fingerprints, doodles by Pickle (yes, a cat draws), and what appears to be the predecessor's handwriting in odd places.

Examples of marginalia:

- Next to the Polish tutorial entry: a small claw scratch and the words *"too vigorous. Approach orb as you would approach a guest at the door — politely."*
- Next to the Cleanse entry: *"this used to terrify me. Now it bores me. I'm not sure which is worse. — M."*
- Next to the Weave entry: *"never weave on a Thursday. — M."* (No explanation. Players will theorize.)
- Next to the Sever entry: an inkblot.
- In the Library: the books occasionally **whisper a sentence** when opened. Some are profound. Some are jokes. (One book whispers *"please put me back upside down. It is more comfortable."*)

The marginalia is **continuous low-grade humor**. The player encounters it ambient. It is the cozy game's smallest, most charming comedic asset class.

## 9.0 Comedy / Grief Co-Existence — the Tannenbaum Rule

> **Rule:** No comedic beat may fire inside a grief beat's "echo radius."

When a sad memory transaction completes — for example, Doris selling Last Light — the next **15 in-game minutes** are a **silent zone.** Pickle is silent. Mishaps are suppressed. Vance, if present, leaves the room without comment. The world holds the moment.

This rule is the difference between a game with humor and **a game where humor punctures grief**. Cult of the Lamb's failure mode is the latter occasionally. We learn from that.

The silent zone is **the most important comedy-side technical discipline** in this codex.

## 10.0 Comedy Toggle — Pickle Sass Intensity

(Per Pell Doyne's request, Codex 06 § 7.) The player can set Pickle's sass intensity 1–5:

| Setting | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 — *Gentle Pickle* | Sass-cleansed. Pickle is observant and warm. No barbs. |
| 2 — *Soft* | Default Gentle Mode setting. Mild observational humor only. |
| 3 — *Default* | The voice bible as written. |
| 4 — *Sharp* | Pickle is sharper. More 12%-rule beats. (Recommended for streamers.) |
| 5 — *Salt* | The unfiltered Pickle. Mild profanity allowed. (Mature-content gated.) |

The 1-toggle audience and the 5-toggle audience are *both* served. This is the difference between a forced tone and a *spectrum.*

## 11.0 Brother Magpie — the Letter-Bird Heckler

The Letter-Bird Network (Codex 15) brings memories between players' villages. One of the local birds — **Brother Magpie** — is a permanent shop-side resident who reads aloud the letters that arrive. His readings include:

- **Editorial commentary.** *"This sender writes with the confidence of a person who has never been edited."*
- **Sotto-voce critique.** *"Penmanship: acceptable. Spelling: heroic in the face of obstacle."*
- **Translation requests.** For letters in foreign languages he loftily declines to read.
- **Refusal to read love letters.** *"I do not perform other people's wantings. Read it yourself."*
- **Occasional poetry-spotting.** He will praise a player's verse if it is good.

Brother Magpie is **the social-feed of the asynchronous co-op.** The community will love him.

## 13.0 The Long Game

Humor is the cozy game's **anti-fatigue layer.** A player who laughs in session 4 will return for session 5. A player who only grieves will, in the median case, return to grieve once and then exit the game. *Spiritfarer's mid-game churn is the textbook example.* We will not repeat it.

Hearthbound Hollow's humor is also **the studio's brand promise**: cozy with edge, cozy with wit, cozy with a *cat that is mostly right.*
