# Hearthbound Hollow
### A Cozy Memory-Broker Narrative Simulation

---

## 1. Vision Statement

> *"In Hearthbound Hollow, every villager has memories they wish they could sell. You are the only one who can buy."*

**Hearthbound Hollow** is a single-player cozy narrative simulation set in a small autumnal village where memories are a physical, tradable commodity. The player inherits the *Hollow*, a memory-brokerage shop that the previous keeper abandoned. They buy painful memories from villagers who want to let go, sell preserved memories to those who long to remember, restore the fragmented, and — sometimes — discover memories that were never meant to be sold.

It's *Stardew Valley*'s warmth, *Spiritfarer*'s emotional weight, *Strange Horticulture*'s tactile investigation, and *Disco Elysium*'s respect for dialogue — at a lighter, cozier register.

## 3. Core Mechanics

### 3.1 The Daily Loop (20–35 min per in-game day)

1. **Morning at the Hollow** – Tend the shop. Polish the memory jars on the shelves. Brew tea.
2. **Open for Business** – Villagers visit. Each has a need: to forget, to remember, to restore, or to *find*.
3. **Memory Work** – At your workbench, manipulate memory orbs through mini-puzzles (sorting, weaving, cleansing).
4. **Village Walk** – Visit the village to deliver, harvest "memory tea" herbs, talk to residents.
5. **Evening Ledger** – Review the day's transactions. Decide which memories to keep, which to log, which to *experience*.
6. **Dream Chapter** – At night, sleep into a "memory dream" — a short, fully-illustrated narrative vignette derived from a memory in your collection.

### 3.2 Pillar Mechanics

#### A) Memory Orbs (Tactile Core)
- Memories take physical form as **glass orbs** of varying colors, sizes, weights, and clarity.
- **Color** = emotional tone (joy, grief, shame, awe, longing, dread, etc.)
- **Clarity** = how intact the memory is
- **Cracks** = trauma; if you "cleanse" without care, you lose the truth
- **Weight** = significance to the giver
- Orbs are stored on shelves. Players physically drag, sort, polish, weave them together.

#### B) The Workbench (Crafting Core)
Four core operations:
1. **Polish** – Restore clarity to a faded orb (mini-game: gentle circular motion)
2. **Cleanse** – Remove cracks by extracting trauma (mini-game: pattern matching). Risk: removing too much erases the memory.
3. **Weave** – Combine two memories into a single, more powerful one (mini-game: tessellation puzzle)
4. **Sever** – Split a complex memory into components (mini-game: reverse tessellation)

Each villager has unique tolerances. Some want their grief cleansed; others would never forgive you for taking it.

#### C) The Village Ledger (Narrative Core)
- Every villager has a hidden **Memory Map** — 12–20 distinct memories that together tell their life story.
- You only see what they choose to sell — *initially*.
- Cross-references between memories reveal connections. Doris's "wedding day" memory contains a face in the crowd that appears in the Mayor's "first regret."
- Solving these connections **unlocks Revelation chapters** — major narrative beats.
- The grand mystery: **why did the previous Hollow-keeper disappear?**

#### D) The Moral Compass (Choice Core)
Unlike farming sims with no moral weight, every memory transaction is a choice:
- A grieving widower wants you to *erase* his wife. Do you do it? Do you keep her memory privately?
- A child wants to *buy* a memory of a father he never had. You have one — from a stranger. Sell it?
- A villager's stolen memory ends up in your shop. Return it to them, profit, or hide it?

Choices ripple. The village evolves over seasons based on whose memories you preserved, altered, or sold to whom.

#### E) Memory Tea & The Garden (Cozy Loop Anchor)
- A small herb garden behind the Hollow. Each herb (lavender, valerian, sage, mugwort, etc.) has a memory-property.
- Brew teas that help villagers open up — or *forget temporarily*.
- This is the comfort/idle loop that lets the game breathe.

## 4. Unique Selling Points

1. **Memories as a physical commodity** — never been done at this depth. Tactile, dramatic, replayable.
2. **A cozy game with real moral weight** — choices matter, villagers remember (or don't).
3. **Every villager is a fully reconstructable mystery** — 30+ residents with hand-authored memory maps.
4. **The Memory Dreams** — branching, illustrated cinematic vignettes triggered by your collection. Free chapter content forever.
5. **A cozy genre with an actual narrative twist** — *who* was the previous Hollow-keeper, and *why* did they leave?
6. **No combat. No death. No grind.** — full positioning against farming-sim genre fatigue.

## 7. Monetization Model

### Base Game: **$24.99**
Premium-cozy bracket (matches Spiritfarer's $29.99 but more competitive). Mobile port $9.99 with same content.

### Expansion Pattern
- **Year 1 Free Seasonal Updates**: Each in-game season adds 1–2 new residents and side-stories
- **Year 2 Paid Story DLC** ($14.99): "The Travelers" — caravan visitors with memories from outside the Hollow
- **Year 3 Paid Story DLC** ($14.99): "The Keeper Before You" — full reveal of predecessor's story
- **Soundtrack & Artbook bundle** ($14.99): cozy audience are heavy collectibles buyers
- **Mobile** $9.99 + optional "Memory Pack" cosmetic shelf decor IAP ($1.99–$4.99)
- **NO**: gacha, energy systems, lootboxes, P2W. Trust is the moat.

## 9. Production Pillars (Dev Constraints)

| Pillar | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Every villager is fully written** | No filler dialog. Every line could be in a novel. |
| **Tactile over textual** | If a feeling can be expressed through orb manipulation, prefer it over text. |
| **Choices don't punish, they shape** | No "wrong" memory transaction. Just consequences. |
| **Cozy framing, deep substance** | Lighting, music, pace stay warm even in heavy moments. |
| **Replayable on emotion, not grind** | New playthroughs explore different residents, not new mechanics. |

## 10. Risk Analysis & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Cozy market saturation | High | Investigation/narrative core differentiates clearly |
| Writing quality bar is *enormous* | High | Hire 2–3 senior narrative designers; prefer fewer/deeper NPCs over breadth |
| Mini-game fatigue | Medium | 4 core ops, each <90s; can be "auto-completed" by skilled players |
| Heavy themes alienate cozy crowd | Medium | Soft framing, optional content warnings, opt-out heavy storylines |
| Mobile cannibalizes console | Low | Mobile is "session" play; full version is "evening" play |
